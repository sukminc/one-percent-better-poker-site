# MVP Report Examples

These examples define the tone and scope for the first paid version of `Exploit Better`.

The promise is simple:

- one tournament
- three habits
- one next-session adjustment

## Example 1

Tournament review:

- You uploaded 1 tournament with 142 hands.
- The field showed frequent limp-calling and loose river calls.

Your 3 habits:

1. You checked back thin value too often against wide callers.
2. You folded too quickly on rivers after passive turn lines.
3. You used standard sizing even when weak players kept showing non-standard lines.

What the table showed:

- This tournament had more abnormal calling behavior than usual.
- Those players kept putting money in with weak ranges, and you beat them often when you value bet clearly.

Next-session adjustment:

1. Against loose callers, look for one extra thin value bet before checking back.

## Example 2

Tournament review:

- You uploaded 1 tournament with 188 hands.
- Most of the pressure came from short-stacked, high-variance opponents.

Your 3 habits:

1. Your aggression dropped after losing a large pot.
2. You passed on isolation spots after limp-heavy opens.
3. You defended too carefully against players showing reckless preflop behavior.

What the table showed:

- Abnormal all-in pressure appeared often in this field.
- Those spots rewarded cleaner punishment more than balanced play.

Next-session adjustment:

1. After a lost pot, keep your next two hands decision-focused and do not tighten by habit.

## Example 3

Tournament review:

- You uploaded 1 tournament with 126 hands.
- This field had repeated overbet patterns from non-standard players.

Your 3 habits:

1. You gave too much credit to oversized river bets.
2. You kept using safe default lines against players who were not balanced.
3. Your value decisions became more passive late in the session.

What the table showed:

- The strange sizing was not random. It repeated in the same player types and often did not represent real strength.

Next-session adjustment:

1. Tag repeated overbet players early and review one call-down spot before your next session starts.
