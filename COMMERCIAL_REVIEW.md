# Commercial Review

## Scope reviewed

- `one-percent-better-poker-site`
- `one-percent-better-poker`
- `one-percent-better-poker-staking` only for context

## Current product promise

The strongest current promise is still:

`One tournament. Three habits. One adjustment.`

In plain language, the offer is:

- upload one GGPoker tournament file
- get a fast pattern read
- leave with one next-session adjustment

That is the sharpest and most sellable version of the product.

## Current CTA

The site currently asks the visitor to do too many different things:

- `See the MVP`
- `Fund the build`
- `Back it`
- `Choose file`
- `Preview sample`

Commercially, the page behaves as if the main CTA is `Fund the build`, but the product-shaped action is actually `Try one tournament now`.

## What is working

- The core energy is strong. `Your pattern. Their leaks. One better session.` feels tighter than a broad poker analytics pitch.
- The live upload/demo panel is the best proof on the page. It makes the product feel real instead of theoretical.
- The structure is already lean. It does not feel bloated or platform-heavy.
- The product repo does contain real proof behind the promise: parser logic, upload handling, a generated review shape, and a tested backend analysis engine.
- The product is differentiated from solver-first tools when it talks about repeated habits and soft-field pattern recognition.

## What feels too broad / too soft / too portfolio-like

- The site does not clearly say who this is for. The real target user exists in the product docs, but the landing page does not plainly say `online MTT players using GGPoker`.
- The main commercial action is unclear. A visitor can upload, preview, read philosophy, open the counter, or fund the build. That splits intent.
- `Fund the build` reads like creator support, not product purchase. It makes the page feel pre-product.
- The philosophy section is good brand texture, but it currently takes space that could be doing more conversion work.
- The product repo still contains portfolio residue: project cards, about/resume surfaces, generic Next.js boilerplate, and older “analytics platform” language.
- The product README and PRD expand into dashboards, growth timelines, fish reports, and broad analytics language. That weakens the simple paid promise.
- The counter is interesting, but on the main product surface it risks making Poker feel like a collection of experiments instead of one offer.

## Review of brand promise vs product proof

The product repo supports the brand promise enough to justify the offer, but only at the `credible MVP` level.

What supports the promise:

- real `.txt` upload handling
- real pattern extraction from uploaded hand histories
- real report structure with habits, field note, and adjustment
- backend endpoints and tests that show real parsing/analytics work exists

What does not yet support a stronger paid claim:

- no clear paid product workflow beyond the first review
- no visible persistent customer history or account-backed memory
- no polished proof that the paid tiers already deliver a materially better report
- too much repo/document language aimed at a future analytics suite

Net: the product can honestly sell `fast first review`. It cannot yet fully sell `complete poker system`.

## What should be cut

- `Fund the build` as the primary above-the-fold action
- extra homepage emphasis on the counter
- any customer-facing copy that sounds like a broad analytics platform
- jargon-heavy feature lists that drift away from the one-tournament review
- portfolio residue if any of it is still reachable from the product repo

## What should be emphasized

- `Upload one GGPoker tournament`
- `See your 3 repeated habits`
- `Leave with 1 next-session adjustment`
- this is for serious online tournament players, not for everyone in poker
- this matters now because most players either study theory or guess; this offers a fast review loop on real play
- the first review is immediate proof, not a long explanation

## Top 5 changes to improve commercial clarity

1. Make `Try one tournament now` the clear primary CTA above the fold.
   Move funding/purchase asks below proof. Let the product action lead.

2. Add one explicit audience line under the hero.
   Example direction: `For online MTT players on GGPoker who want a fast post-session pattern read.`

3. Tighten the hero/subhead around the strongest promise.
   Keep the current energy, but make the utility unmistakable: upload one file, get three habits, take one adjustment.

4. Replace part of the philosophy weight with proof weight.
   Show one concrete sample output, supported file type, and what the free review includes. Conversion will beat abstraction here.

5. Treat the counter as secondary until it has its own commercial role.
   Keep it available, but do not let it compete with the main upload-review offer on the homepage.

## Smallest useful messaging / structure improvements

- Rename the free demo CTA cluster so it reads like one path: `Try free review`, `Upload tournament`, `See sample report`.
- Reframe paid tiers as paying for more review depth or repeat review, not as “backing the build.”
- Keep `One tournament. Three habits. One adjustment.` as the organizing idea. It is stronger than the broader feature language.
- If philosophy stays, compress it. It should support resonance, not compete with the offer.
- In product docs and repo copy, replace broad “platform” framing with `poker review tool` or `pattern review tool`.

## Bottom line

The commercial opportunity is already visible, but the page is one decision away from being much sharper:

sell the first review, not the build.

Everything that makes Poker feel like a side lab, creator page, or future platform should be demoted behind that core promise.
